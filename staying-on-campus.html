Air Conditioning

Seattle is famous for its rain, but visitors are often surprised to find that most of our summer days are warm and sunny! Although the average temperature is in the low 70s, it is possible for some weeks of summer to have temperatures reaching 90°F or higher.

Residence halls and many campus building do not have air-conditioning. Ask us about your options for beating the heat! Fans may be rented at the front desk while supplies last (see Amenities). Guests may also access the air-conditioned Student Center with their Conference Card and our recreational swimming pool is popular during the summer months (see Fitness Center).

ALCOHOL

Only those guests 21 years of age and older are permitted to consume alcoholic beverages in private rooms of residences only. Alcoholic beverages may not be consumed in lounge areas of residence halls or anywhere else outside the residences, except in arranged program events with appropriate permits.

CHECK-IN AND CHECK-OUT

Check-in starts at 1pm
Check-out is by 10am

For check-out: all belongings must be removed from your room and your key must be returned to the front desk by 10 a.m. on your day of check out. Limited luggage storage is available at the front desk. Overnight luggage storage is not available. Guests will be subject to a $25.00 fee for late check-out.

HOUSEKEEPING

Programs longer than seven nights will receive room service once a week on a pre-arranged schedule. This includes fresh linens and towels and emptying of waste bins. Guests must remove belongings from beds so the bed can be made.

INTERNET ACCESS

A username and password are required to access both hardwired and wireless SU networks. WIRELESS ACCESS: Devices may connect using “SU-guest” network and your username and password. WIRED ACCESS:  Guests must provide their own Ethernet cable and plug it into one of their rooms network ports/jacks For assistance contact your front desk or call the IT Helpdesk at 206-296-5571. Wireless and network access is complimentary. Front desk staff and/or IT Helpdesk staff may not always be able to resolve connectivity issues and therefore service cannot always be guaranteed. For more detailed information on connecting to the internet click here.

PARKING

Parking passes are required for all vehicles and may be purchased at the front desk of your housing accommodations for $6.00 per vehicle, per day. Visitors may also pay to park at the meter in the visitor lot on the lower level of the Murphy Garage or in the Visitor Lot at 12th Avenue and E. Marion Street. Vehicles parked without a valid permit will be cited and fined. Seattle University is not responsible for loss, theft, damage to vehicles or their contents, or parking fines. Vehicles that are parked in non-designated areas will be towed at the owner’s expense.
