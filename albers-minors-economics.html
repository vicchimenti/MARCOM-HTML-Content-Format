To earn a minor in economics, students must complete thirty (30) credits of economics, including the following:
Course NumberCourse TitleCredits
ECON 2110 Principles of Economics-Micro 5
ECON 2130 Principles of Economics-Macro 5
ECON 3110 Intermediate Microeconomics 5
ECON 3130 Global and Domestic Macroeconomics 5
ECON Electives 3000-4000 level (see advisor) 10

Please note:

  1. Students working toward a minor in economics are subject to the same grade requirements as students pursuing a major in economics.
  2. FINC 3430 and ECON 4890 will not satisfy the upper division economics electives. See policy for minors in Academic Policies section of the Bulletin.
